Meet Amy, a cooking enthusiastic

How it all began

As a kid, I was the pickiest eater. Even though I was chubby baby, I soon lost all that extra weight and became very slim, opting in to play rather than to eat. However, this all changed when I was in 3rd grade.

My mom got really sick one day, and I had to scramble to figure out food options for myself. As a result, I had to eat instant ramen every day. And I soon was so hungry that I would even eat shrimp (I hated seafood at that time).

Fast forward to high school, I became a great sue chef by helping my sickly mom to cook everyday. I became well-versed in the so-called "eyeballing" method when it came to a lot of homecooked chinese dishes. But what really accelerated the growth was when COVID started. I suddenly found myself with a lot more time, and wanting to make the mundane days more interesting, I also started experiementing with cooking new recipes. And I loved it so much that I never stopped! Hence the beginnings of my foodstagram.

Development of Foodstagram

As the name states, a foodstagram is an Instagram that features food. My handle is @amyfannliu, which is my personal IG but I mainly only feature food that I made. There are no repeats and they feature the dishes that I've tried to make for the first time.

Since I started this, I have had over 250+ posts, with 500+ followers (mainly my friends, as my IG is private). I am not interested in making this a public IG since my goal is more for personal documenting rather than amassing a huge following. In addition to food, I also occasionally post some of the art pieces that I have created as well.

Some cuisines that I have made include: Chinese, Japanese, Thai, Korean, Vietnemese, American, Indian, Mexican, Dominican, Italian, Fusion, African, etc. These are not limited to main courses, but also include side dishes, appetizers, desserts, as well as drinks.

Looking Ahead

As I grow older, I still love to experiement and try out new recipes. I am always open to suggestions, and sometimes I get inspired by the food I had a particular restaurant, and would go out of my way to replicate it. One example is the melted cheese over a rich kalbijjim (Korean braised beef short rib stew) from Daeho, I have been able to create my own spicy version mixed in with sweet potato noodles.

All of that goes to say that I am still open to suggestions! If there is anything you want to see me try to make, feel free to submit in my form above!
